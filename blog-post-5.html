What is Form IEPF-5? Complete Guide

February 20, 2026 3 min read By Sharesuraksha Team

Form IEPF-5 is the official application form for claiming shares, dividends, and other investments that have been transferred to the Investor Education and Protection Fund (IEPF). Here's everything you need to know.

What is IEPF-5?

Form IEPF-5 is prescribed under Rule 7(1) of the Investor Education and Protection Fund Authority (Accounting, Audit, Transfer and Refund) Rules, 2016. It's the primary document for claiming:

  • Shares transferred to IEPF
  • Unclaimed dividends
  • Matured deposits/debentures
  • Interest on above

When to File IEPF-5?

File IEPF-5 when:

  • Your shares/dividends were transferred to IEPF (after 7 years of being unclaimed)
  • You haven't claimed dividends for 7+ consecutive years
  • You're a legal heir claiming deceased's shares from IEPF

Step-by-Step IEPF-5 Filing Process

Step 1: Online Filing

Visit the MCA portal (www.mca.gov.in) and:

  • Register as a user
  • Select Form IEPF-5
  • Fill personal and claim details
  • Upload required documents
  • Submit and get acknowledgement SRN

Step 2: Download and Print

After online submission, download:

  • Filled IEPF-5 form
  • Acknowledgement slip
  • Indemnity bond (generated by system)

Step 3: Physical Submission

Send physical documents to company's Nodal Officer within 90 days:

  • Signed IEPF-5 form
  • Original acknowledgement
  • Indemnity bond (notarized)
  • All supporting documents
SRN (Service Request Number) is crucial for tracking your claim.

Key Sections of IEPF-5

Part A: Basic Details

  • Name of claimant
  • Father's/Spouse name
  • Address, PAN, Aadhaar
  • Contact details

Part B: Claim Details

  • Company name and CIN
  • Folio number/DP ID-Client ID
  • Number of shares/amount claimed
  • Reason for claim

Part C: Bank Details

  • Bank name, branch, IFSC
  • Account number
  • Account type

Part D: Verification

  • Declaration and signature
  • Place and date

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Wrong CIN

Company Identification Number must be exact. Verify from company website or MCA portal.

Mistake 2: Mismatched Signatures

Signatures across all documents must match. Get bank verification if needed.

Mistake 3: Missing Attachments

Every document listed must be attached. Missing documents cause rejection.

Mistake 4: Wrong Stamp Paper Value

Indemnity bond must be on proper value stamp paper (varies by state).

After Submission

  • Company Verification: Company verifies documents (2-4 weeks)
  • IEPF Approval: IEPF Authority reviews and approves
  • Transfer: Shares to demat, dividends to bank (4-8 weeks)
Total Time: 3-6 months from filing to credit.
