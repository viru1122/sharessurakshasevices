How to Claim Unclaimed Dividends from IEPF

February 15, 2026 5 min read By Sharesuraksha Team

Did you know that over ₹38,000 crores in corporate dividends are lying unclaimed? If you've missed dividend payments for 7+ years, they're now in IEPF. Here's how to claim them back.

Understanding Unclaimed Dividends

When a company declares a dividend and you don't claim it:

  • First 7 years: Dividends stay with the company as "unclaimed dividend"
  • After 7 years: Transferred to IEPF along with shares
  • Interest: No interest accrues after transfer to IEPF

How to Check if You Have Unclaimed Dividends

Method 1: IEPF Website

Visit www.iepf.gov.in and click "Search Unclaimed and Unpaid Amounts". Search by:

  • Name
  • Folio number
  • PAN (if available)
  • Company name

Method 2: Company Websites

Many companies list unclaimed dividend data in their investor relations section.

Method 3: Contact Us

We can do a comprehensive search across all companies for you.

Claim Process for Unclaimed Dividends

If Dividends Are Still with Company (<7 years)

  1. Contact company's registrar (RTA)
  2. Submit dividend claim form
  3. Provide bank proof and ID
  4. Dividend credited directly to bank

If Dividends Are in IEPF (>7 years)

Follow the IEPF claim process:

Step 1: File Form IEPF-5

  • Visit MCA portal (www.mca.gov.in)
  • Fill Form IEPF-5 online
  • Mention dividend amount and financial years
  • Get acknowledgement SRN

Step 2: Prepare Physical Documents

  • Printed and signed IEPF-5
  • Acknowledgement slip
  • Indemnity bond (notarized)
  • Advance stamped receipt
  • PAN and Aadhaar copies
  • Bank proof (cancelled cheque)
  • Original dividend warrants (if available)

Step 3: Submit to Company

Send all documents to company's Nodal Officer within 90 days of online filing.

Dividends are paid directly to your bank account once approved by IEPF.

Documents Required for Dividend Claims

Document Purpose
Form IEPF-5 Main claim form
PAN Card Identity proof
Aadhaar Card Address proof
Cancelled Cheque Bank account proof
Indemnity Bond Legal protection
Dividend Warrants Supporting proof (if available)

Common Issues in Dividend Claims

Issue 1: Multiple Financial Years

You need to claim all unclaimed years together. List all years in the form.

Issue 2: Company Merged/Demerger

Claim from the current entity. Provide merger documents if required.

Issue 3: Name Changes

If your name changed, provide supporting documents (marriage certificate, etc.).

Issue 4: Legal Heir Claims

For deceased shareholder's dividends, provide succession certificate and death certificate.

Timeline for Dividend Claims

  • Company Verification: 2-3 weeks
  • IEPF Processing: 4-6 weeks
  • Payment Transfer: 1-2 weeks
  • Total: 2-3 months
Tax Implication: Dividends are taxable in your hand for the year you receive them. TDS may apply.

Prevent Future Unclaimed Dividends

  • Keep your contact details updated with companies
  • Provide email and mobile for dividend alerts
  • Convert physical shares to demat
  • Enable ECS (electronic) dividend payment
  • Check dividend status annually
