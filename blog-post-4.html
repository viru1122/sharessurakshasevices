How to Convert Physical Shares to Demat Form

February 28, 2026 4 min read By Sharesuraksha Team

Physical share certificates are becoming obsolete. SEBI has made demat shares mandatory for transfer and sale. Here's your complete guide to converting physical shares to demat form.

Why Convert to Demat?

  • Safety: No risk of loss, theft, or damage
  • Easy Transfer: Instant transfer and sale
  • No Stamp Duty: Save on transfer costs
  • Prevents IEPF Transfer: Active demat accounts prevent shares from going to IEPF

Documents Required

  • Original physical share certificates
  • Demat account details (Client ID, DP ID)
  • PAN Card copy
  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport)
  • Proof of address
  • Recent passport size photographs

Step-by-Step Demat Process

Step 1: Open a Demat Account

If you don't have one, open a demat account with any SEBI-registered depository participant (bank, broker, or financial institution).

Step 2: Submit Demat Request Form (DRF)

Get Form DRF from your DP. Fill it with details:

  • Company name and script details
  • Number of shares
  • Certificate numbers and distinctive numbers
  • Folio number

Step 3: Submit Certificates

Submit original share certificates along with DRF to your DP. Ensure certificates are in good condition.

Step 4: Verification by RTA

Your DP sends certificates to company's Registrar and Transfer Agent (RTA) for verification. RTA checks:

  • Certificate authenticity
  • Signature verification
  • No stop transfer instructions

Step 5: Demat Credit

After verification (typically 15-30 days), shares are credited to your demat account.

Total time: 30-45 days depending on RTA response and document accuracy.

Common Issues and Solutions

1. Signature Mismatch

If signatures on certificates don't match current records, provide:

  • Signature verification from bank
  • Affidavit explaining variation
  • Old signed documents for comparison

2. Name Mismatch

For name changes due to marriage or legal reasons, provide:

  • Marriage certificate or gazette notification
  • Affidavit for name change

3. Lost Certificates

If certificates are lost, you need:

  • FIR copy (police complaint)
  • Indemnity bond on stamp paper
  • Affidavit of loss
  • Newspaper publication (in some cases)

Charges for Demat Conversion

  • DP Charges: ₹50-100 per certificate + GST
  • Courier Charges: Actual (₹50-100 per request)
  • RTA Fees: Included in DP charges usually
  • Stamp Duty: Nominal (varies by state)
Important Deadline: Physical shares cannot be transferred or sold after certain deadlines. Convert early to maintain liquidity.

Special Cases

Joint Holdings

All joint holders need to sign DRF. If any holder deceased, transmission process needed first.

Multiple Certificates

You can submit multiple certificates from same company in one DRF, saving time and charges.

Company Merged or Name Changed

Shares of merged companies can still be dematted. RTA will verify current status.
