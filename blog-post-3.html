How to Claim Shares of Deceased Family Member

March 5, 2026 6 min read By Sharesuraksha Team

Losing a loved one is difficult, and dealing with legal matters afterward can be overwhelming. If your family member held shares that are now unclaimed, here's a complete guide on how to claim them.

Understanding Transmission of Shares

When a shareholder passes away, their shares need to be "transmitted" to the legal heirs. This process differs from regular share transfer and has its own set of rules and requirements.

Two Scenarios Based on Share Type

Scenario 1: Physical Share Certificates

If the shares are in physical form, you'll need to approach the company's registrar with:

  • Death certificate (original + attested copy)
  • Succession certificate or probate of will
  • Indemnity bond on stamp paper
  • Affidavit of legal heirs
  • PAN cards of all legal heirs
  • Bank account proofs

Scenario 2: Demat Shares

For shares in demat form, contact the depository participant (DP) with:

  • Transmission request form
  • Death certificate
  • Succession certificate or probate
  • Surviving shareholder's ID proof (for joint holdings)

Key Documents Explained

1. Death Certificate

Issued by municipal corporation or registrar of births and deaths. Must be original or certified copy.

2. Succession Certificate

Obtained from civil court. Required when there's no will or when shares are significant. Process takes 3-6 months.

3. Probate of Will

If the deceased left a will, probate from high court validates it. Required for shares above certain value.

4. Legal Heir Certificate

For smaller claims, some companies accept legal heir certificate from tehsildar or magistrate.

Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Gather All Documents

Collect all mentioned documents. Make multiple copies. Keep originals safe.

Step 2: Identify All Shares

Search for all shares held by the deceased. Check old files, lockers, and contact companies directly.

Step 3: Apply for Transmission

Submit transmission request to each company's registrar or DP along with documents.

Step 4: Follow Up

Companies may take 30-60 days to process. Regular follow-up is essential.

Important: If shares are already transferred to IEPF, you'll need to file Form IEPF-5 as legal heir with additional documents.

Special Cases

Joint Holdings

If shares were held jointly, they typically transfer to surviving joint holder(s) with simplified process.

Minor Legal Heirs

If legal heir is minor, a natural guardian must apply. Court-appointed guardian may be required for significant value.

Multiple Legal Heirs

All heirs need to apply jointly or provide No Objection Certificates from others.

Remember: Shares can be claimed even if transferred to IEPF. The process is longer but definitely possible.
